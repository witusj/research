Probability

Probability Theory

A framework for analyzing phenomena with uncertain outcomes

  • Rules for concistent reasoning

  • Used for predictions and decisions

Role of Probability Theory

Ingredients Probability Calculation

  • Sample space

  • Probability law

  • Event

Sample Space

  • List (set) of all possible outcomes, \(\Omega\)
  • The elements of the sample space are MECE

Probablity Law

Description of beliefs about likelihood of different possible outcomes

Event

  • A subset of the sample space

  • Probability is assigned to events

Probability Axioms

  • \(P(A) \geq0\)

  • \(P(\Omega) = 1\)

  • \(If \ A \cap B = \emptyset\)
    \(\Rightarrow P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)\)

Complement of Set

\[ A \cup A^C = \Omega \]


\[ A \cap A^C = \emptyset \]

Disjoint Sets

Conditional Probabilities

Assume 12 equally likely outcomes
\(P(A) = \frac{5}{12}, P(B) = \frac{6}{12}\)

If told B occured
\(P(A|B) = \frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{3}, P(B|B) = 1\)

Definition of Conditional Probability

\(P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}\\Defined\ only\ if\ P(B) > 0\)

\(P(A \cap B) = \frac{2}{12}, P(B) = \frac{6}{12}\)
